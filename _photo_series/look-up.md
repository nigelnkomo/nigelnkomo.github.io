This series began as an exercise in attention, forcing myself to look up from the street level where I typically walk, absorbed in the ground and the path ahead. What I found was a hidden vocabulary of architectural ambition: decorative cornices that once spoke of prosperity, geometric window patterns that once caught afternoon light deliberately, ironwork that once announced the taste of the occupant.

The buildings are old now, many crumbling, the ground beneath them often neglected and uninspiring. But the facades tell a different story of what this city once aspired to be, and perhaps could be again.
